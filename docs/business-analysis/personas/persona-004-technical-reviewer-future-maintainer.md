## Profile

The Technical Reviewer or Future Maintainer is a developer or stakeholder who revisits the project after the prototype is built. They may be the original Solo Builder returning later, a new contributor, or another Codex session. They need to understand the project, run it locally, validate its quality, examine decisions, and continue development.

## Goals

- Understand what the prototype is, why it exists, and how it works.
- Quickly grasp product brief, scope, architecture, and major decisions.
- Run the prototype locally with minimal setup friction.
- Validate the prototype’s current quality using tests and checks.
- Review known limitations and future backlog.
- Continue work from the current state with confidence.

## Pain Points

- AI‑generated projects may lack coherent documentation.
- Decisions can be hidden in chat history or comments.
- Setup instructions may be incomplete or outdated.
- Test results may be unclear or missing.
- Known limitations may not be documented.
- Repo state may be difficult to resume after context has been lost.

## Needs

- A product brief, approved scope, and solution design documents.
- ADRs for significant decisions.
- A clear backlog and sprint history.
- Up‑to‑date test reports and smoke test instructions.
- A final handoff document summarising limitations and future work.
- A compact project memory summarising current state.

## Notes

This persona emphasises maintainability and transparency. A well‑structured project is easier to review, hand over, and evolve.
