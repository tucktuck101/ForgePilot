## Summary

ForgePilot shall require human approval of the prototype scope before any implementation begins. The scope document outlines what features will be built, what is excluded, and acceptance criteria for the prototype.

## Details

After the product brief is approved, Codex should:

- Present the scope document to the user, detailing in-scope features, out-of-scope items, and acceptance criteria.
- Ask for approval to confirm this scope as the basis for the first build.
- Record the approval decision and revise the scope if necessary.

Implementation work on the prototype should not start until the scope is confirmed.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Codex requests approval for the scope document.
- The user’s decision is recorded (approved/rejected/revisions needed).
- Approved scope guides subsequent backlog and sprint planning.

## Notes

This requirement ensures clarity on what the prototype will include, preventing scope creep and misaligned expectations.
