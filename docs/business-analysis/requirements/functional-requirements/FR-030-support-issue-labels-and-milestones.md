## Summary

ForgePilot should leverage GitHub labels and milestones to organise and prioritise issues when appropriate. Labels and milestones help group related tasks and indicate status or priority.

## Details

- Codex may assign labels like `bug`, `enhancement`, `documentation`, `prototype`, or `approved` to issues.
- Milestones can represent larger goals, such as “Initial Prototype”, “MVP Release”, or “Stabilisation Phase”.
- When labels or milestones exist in the repository, Codex should assign them based on the nature of each issue.
- The use of labels should remain consistent and documented in the repository’s contribution guidelines or as part of the template.

## Acceptance Criteria

- Issues generated by Codex include appropriate labels when relevant.
- Milestones are created or used to track major delivery phases.
- Label use is consistent across issues.

## Notes

While labels and milestones are optional, they can greatly improve organisation and clarity, especially on larger projects. The initial template may define a default set of labels.
