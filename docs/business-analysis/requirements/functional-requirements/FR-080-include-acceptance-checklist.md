## Summary

The final handoff must include an acceptance checklist to guide the final review process.

## Details

To support a structured acceptance review, the final handoff should provide a checklist summarising the key aspects that the user or client should verify before accepting the prototype. The checklist may include verification of functionality against approved scope, confirmation of test results, review of security/privacy notes, confirmation of deployment readiness, and acceptance or rejection of known limitations. Each item should allow the reviewer to mark it as complete or needing attention.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The handoff document contains a clearly identifiable acceptance checklist.
- Checklist items cover functionality, tests, docs, deployment readiness, known limitations, and next steps.
- The checklist allows the reviewer to record acceptance decisions or comments.

## Notes

An acceptance checklist helps ensure that final approval is systematic and comprehensive.
