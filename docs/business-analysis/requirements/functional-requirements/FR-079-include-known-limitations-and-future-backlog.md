## Summary

The final handoff must list known limitations and a future backlog.

## Details

After completing a prototype, it is essential to document any outstanding limitations, deferred features, or issues that could not be addressed within the initial scope. The final handoff should include a section that details these known limitations and points to a future backlog for further development. The backlog can reference GitHub Issues or a separate section in the handoff document. This transparency helps reviewers and future maintainers understand the maturity of the prototype and plan subsequent work.

## Acceptance Criteria

- The handoff document contains a section titled "Known Limitations" describing known bugs, missing features, or design compromises.
- A "Future Backlog" section lists tasks that are intentionally deferred, with links to corresponding GitHub Issues if available.
- Limitations and backlog items are clearly distinguished from the completed scope.

## Notes

Explicitly documenting limitations and future work fosters transparency and sets expectations for next steps.
